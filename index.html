Chapter 9: Friction

Friction is a force that opposes the motion of an object over another surface.

  • Static Friction: Acts when an object is at rest.
  • Sliding Friction: Acts when an object slides over another.
  • Rolling Friction: Acts when an object rolls. (Rolling is easier than sliding!)
Tip: Friction can be increased by making surfaces rough (tires) or decreased by using lubricants (oil/grease).

Chapter 10: Light

Light allows us to see the world. It follows the Law of Reflection.

  • Angle of Incidence ($\angle i$) is always equal to the Angle of Reflection ($\angle r$).
  • Human Eye: The lens focuses light on the Retina, which sends signals to the brain via the Optic Nerve.
